# CIC Plan for an Educational Community Interest Company (CIC) in the UK

## **1. Define Your Educational Mission**
- Specify the target audience: youth, adults, or underserved communities.
- Decide on the educational focus: academic tutoring, vocational training, digital skills, or personal development.
- Clarify whether you will offer online courses, workshops, or physical training centers.

---

## **2. Align with CIC Requirements**
- Ensure the educational initiative benefits the community and meets CIC guidelines.
- Profits must be reinvested into the education cause or donated to support educational projects.

---

## **3. Prepare the Required Documents**
1. **CIC36 Form:**  
   - Explain how your business activities (like selling educational services/products) will benefit the community.  
   - Provide examples of the social impact, such as improved employment prospects or digital literacy.

2. **Memorandum and Articles of Association:**  
   - Include clauses on the asset lock and reinvestment of profits to promote education.

---

## **4. Register with Companies House**
- Submit the CIC36 form and Articles of Association.
- Pay the registration fee (£27 online or £35 by post).

---

## **5. Develop Educational Products/Services**
- Create learning resources, classes, or workshops aligned with your mission.
- Consider partnerships with educational platforms or professionals.

---

## **6. Financial and Governance Setup**
- Open a business bank account for the CIC.
- Appoint at least one director.

---

## **7. Compliance and Reporting**
- Submit annual accounts and a **Community Interest Report** to demonstrate how your activities benefited the educational community.

---

## **8. Marketing and Impact Building**
- Highlight the CIC status to build trust and credibility.
- Share success stories about learners who benefited from your services.
